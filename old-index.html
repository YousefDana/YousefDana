IDOT Safety Analysis

Transportation Analysis

Yousef provided technical and analysis support to further develop the Illinois Safety Improvement Program (ILSP). Relevant programs and applications used were ArcMap, Visual Basic, Microsoft Access and Excel. Tasks ranged from geolocating projects, assigning crashes and conducting safety analysis. Each of the three phases used geographic information systems (GIS) to analyze crash and roadway data.

The Top 50 Curves with Safety Improvement Potential project aimed to provide IDOT with nine maps for each district displaying the highest ranked 50 curved roadways within Illinois. The findings in this report were used to help safety professionals prioritize improving curves. Approximately 525 curves were geospatially identified and analyzed to rank curves based on a crash severity weighting scheme and environment factors.

The Illinois Crash Rate Heat Map project aimed to provide IDOT with 9792 GIS maps displaying the rate of crashes within each county in Illinois. Each county was provided with 96 maps. Four different rates for 24 different emphasis areas that the county intends to analyze. Emphasis areas range from a drunk driving crash to a crashes relative to age. The rates analyzed were roadway departure crashes, roadway departure crash rate, total KABC injuries, and total KABC crash rate. The calculation was conducted using the python and ArcMap. This project utilized ArcMap’s data driven pages and python scripting to produce 9792 maps automatically.

  • Date: 2018-2019
  • Client: Illinois Department of Transportaton, Milhouse Engineering, Jacobs Engineering
  • Category: Analysis / Forecasting

IDOT Safety Analysis

Transportation Analysis, Reporting, and Forecasting

The Highway Safety Improvement Program is a data-driven, strategic approach intended to produce measurable and significant reduction in traffic related fatalities. Under Milhouse Engineering, my role in the program is to generate data-driven exhibits using Python, GIS, VBA, and Microsoft Access. Exhibits range from determining intersections with most improvement potential to determining the percentage success of a safety improvement project.

Yousef provided technical, engineering, and analysis support to further develop the Illinois Safety Improvement Program (ILSP). Relevant programs and applications used were ArcMap, Visual Basic, Microsoft Access, and Excel. Tasks ranged from geolocating projects, assigning crashes and conducting safety analysis. Each of the three phases used geographic information systems (GIS) to analyze crash and roadway data. HSIP, T50CSIP, and CRHM.

The Highway Safety Improvement Program (HSIP) Performance Tracking Tool aimed to provide the Illinois Department of Transportation (IDOT) with an analysis on the success of safety improvement intended projects within a 3 year span from a project construction date. Approximately 800 HSIP projects were located analyzed in ArcMap. Crashes occurring before construction and after construction were spatially assigned in GIS to each of the geolocated projects for use in project evaluation. A tracking tool was developed in Microsoft Excel to rank the success of a safety improvement project. Tools used were GIS Spatial Joins, Microsoft Access Queries, and Microsoft Excel Pivot Tables.

  • Date: 2017-2018
  • Client: Illinois Department of Transportaton, Milhouse Engineering, Jacobs Engineering
  • Category: Analysis / Forecasting
